Mollie Marshall

A woman who embodied kindness and understanding
August 10, 1939 - June 25, 2018

The Story of Mollie's Birth

Mollie enjoyed relaying her family's story of her birth. She was told that her mother and her aunt were sitting outside on a hot summer day drinking mint julep tea. They heard a series of strange noises coming from the heavens, and eventually they could make out a giant bird descending through the treetops toward them. The bird had a small package tied to its beak, and when they came closer, they saw that it was a precious little baby. They took the baby and began to argue about what to name it. Her mother wanted to name her "Mollie," while her aunt wanted to name her "Nell," so they settled on calling her Mollie Nell Mims.

Mollie's Professional Legacy

A lifelong resident of Jasper, Texas, Mollie was born on August 10, 1938 to Herman & Blanche Mims. After graduating from Northwestern University, she became an educator at Jasper ISD, specializing in science, until her retirement in 1996. Following her retirement, Mollie worked for Jasper Memorial Hospital as a compliance and lab supervisor until April 2018.
